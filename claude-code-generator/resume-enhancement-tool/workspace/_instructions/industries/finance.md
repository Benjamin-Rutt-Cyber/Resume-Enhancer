# Finance Industry Resume Guidelines

## Overview

Finance resumes should emphasize quantifiable financial impact, analytical skills, regulatory knowledge, and relevant certifications. Numbers, ROI, and financial metrics are critical.

## Resume Structure

1. **Header** (Name, Contact Info, LinkedIn)
2. **Professional Summary** (Quantified achievements)
3. **Core Competencies**
4. **Professional Experience** (metrics-heavy)
5. **Education** (GPA if > 3.5)
6. **Certifications** (CFA, CPA, FRM, etc.)
7. **Technical Skills**
8. **Additional** (Languages, Publications)

## Key Keywords

**Financial Analysis:** Financial Modeling, Valuation, DCF Analysis, Forecasting, Budgeting, Variance Analysis
**Accounting:** GAAP, IFRS, Financial Reporting, Auditing, Reconciliation, Month-End Close
**Investment:** Portfolio Management, Asset Allocation, Risk Management, Due Diligence, M&A
**Systems:** Bloomberg Terminal, SAP, Oracle Financials, QuickBooks, Excel (Advanced), Power BI, Tableau
**Regulations:** SEC, SOX, Dodd-Frank, Basel III, FINRA

## Quantification Examples

- "Managed portfolio of $250M in assets, achieving 12% annual return vs 8% benchmark"
- "Reduced operational costs by $2.3M (15%) through process optimization and vendor renegotiation"
- "Led financial due diligence for $50M acquisition, identifying $5M in potential cost synergies"
- "Improved forecast accuracy from 82% to 96% by implementing advanced predictive modeling"

## Common Certifications

- CFA (Chartered Financial Analyst)
- CPA (Certified Public Accountant)
- FRM (Financial Risk Manager)
- CMA (Certified Management Accountant)
- Series 7, Series 63, Series 65
